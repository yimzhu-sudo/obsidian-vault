# NDIS Deep Research — June 2026

**Date researched:** 14 June 2026
**Tags:** `#ndis` `#disability` `#australian-politics` `#policy` `#social-services`

---

## 1. The Big Picture

The NDIS now supports **~760,000 participants** at a cost of **$50+ billion per year** — more expensive than Medicare, approaching defence spending. Growing at **10.3% annually** (down from 22% in 2022). Without intervention, projected to cost **$117B/year within a decade**.

**Budget 2026-27** expects **$37.8B in savings over four years** from the reforms.

---

## 2. The Landmark Bill: "Securing the NDIS for Future Generations"

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Full name** | National Disability Insurance Scheme Amendment (Securing the NDIS for Future Generations) Bill 2026 |
| **Introduced** | 14 May 2026 to Parliament |
| **Passed House** | 28 May 2026 |
| **Status** | Under Senate inquiry (hearings 9–11 June 2026) |
| **Goal** | Return NDIS to "original intent" — permanent and significant disability only |

### Four Pillars:
1. **Quality services** — commission plan management & support coordination; consult on commissioning model for Supported Independent Living (SIL)
2. **Clearer eligibility** — standardised, evidence-based functional assessments
3. **Slow cost growth** — tighter plan reassessments, reset social/community participation budgets, New Framework Planning from April 2027
4. **Anti-fraud** — stronger provider oversight, investigative powers, regulatory controls

---

## 3. Contentious Items in the Bill

### 3.1 Sweeping Ministerial Powers
The minister can reduce funding for categories of support "to ensure financial sustainability." Planned cuts:
- **Social & community participation budgets** → **50% reduction** (support workers for doctor visits, shopping)
- **Capacity building budgets** → **10% reduction** (therapies)
- Minister can cap hourly rates, staff-to-participant ratios, and total funding amounts

> "It will allow this and any future minister effectively to have the PIN to the bank account for the NDIS." — Naomi Anderson, Villamanta Disability Rights Legal Service

### 3.2 Tightened Eligibility
- Access based on **"substantially reduced functional capacity"** (severe disability)
- Participants must exhaust **"all appropriate treatments"** — even if cost or location prevents access
- Described as a **"reverse means test"** by government's own advisory committee
- Diagnosis lists removed as entry method

### 3.3 Automated Decision-Making
- Computer programs could make administrative decisions including rejecting claims
- Minister can expand automation powers unilaterally

## 5. Fraud & Integrity Crackdown

### Scale:
- **$3.7B** lost to "integrity leakage" last year — **8.3% of $45B** in payments
- **90% of claims had no evidence** behind them (Mark Butler)
- QSC (watchdog) not established until 2018 — 5 years after scheme launched
- Up to **20%+** of funding may be exploited by criminals (2024 estimate)

### Fraud Fusion Taskforce Results (est. 2022):
- **660 active investigations**
- **59 people referred to court** → **25 convictions**
- **77 warrants** last year (vs. 30 combined across 2018–2021)
- **2,500+ providers** removed or disrupted

### Systemic Failures ID'd:
- Rollout was "rushed" — too many unverified payment claims
- Enforcement "lacking" — integrity not resourced
- Focus on reaction instead of prevention
- "Lessons from earlier government programs were repeated" — integrity chief John Dardo

## 6. State Governments Push Back

All state and territory disability ministers issued a **joint submission** to Senate inquiry:
- 300,000 expected exits **not matched with alternative supports**
- "Pace of reform focused heavily on expenditure constraint"
- **"Significant risk people with disability will end up in hospitals or have no access at all"**
- Called for "genuine co-governance"

---

## 7. Advocacy Sector Concerns

- **PWDA**: Reforms roll out 2026–2028, but major details remain unclear. New Foundational Supports & Thriving Kids ($2B + $5B matched) — not yet built.
- **ACT Disability Caucus**: "Reform that removes support before alternatives exist is not reform" — it's **displacing risk onto people with disability**
- **Disability Advocacy Network Australia**: States will "carry the consequences of unmet need"
